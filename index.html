Magnolia Populations in Ecuador

Link to a higher resolution map.

Authored by: Bailey Vernon

This map was created using QGIS 3.6 and macOS Catalina. It has a cordinate reference system of ESRI: 102033. When thinking about how to display population density, a heatmap lends itself well to visual representation. Endgangered magnolia species span the world, with locations in China and the US, but a majority of them survive within the South American country of Ecuador. This concentration is due to climate and habitat, as well as more isolation than other global locations. 75% of engangered magnolias live in this country, and within about 10 of the 24 provinces, showing just how particular the natural world can be. There is a focus in the middle of the country, where there is less volatility from the coast and located near preserved national parks. More information about this map and its metadata can be found here, and this map was published on June 15, 2022.
